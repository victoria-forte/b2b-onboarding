Your 30 / 60 / 90 Day Ramp

Based on Revenue Onboarding and the AE Handbook expectations.

Days 1–30 — Learn & Absorb

Revenue Onboarding is your foundation. ~50% of your time in weeks 1–2 should be on async modules and live sessions. The B2B pathway covers product features, positioning, pricing, competitive landscape, and value selling.

  • Complete all 6 Revenue Onboarding modules (B2B pathway ~17 hrs)
  • Shadow experienced reps — calls, demos, deal reviews
  • Set up Salesforce, Salesloft, Copilot, Seismic, LinkedIn Sales Nav
  • Assess your book of business and tier accounts
  • Get assigned a real deliverable by end of week 2
  • New Hire Shadowing sign-off from manager or buddy

Days 31–60 — Execute & Build Pipeline

Transition from learning to doing. Own your pipeline, run outbound sequences, and start running discovery calls with SE support.

  • Process inbound leads within 30 days (speed to lead: 24 hrs)
  • Launch Salesloft outbound sequences (email + phone + LinkedIn)
  • Run discovery calls — document in Salesforce with SFDC stages
  • Build toward 3x pipeline coverage
  • Attend Startup Connections (in-person, required within 90 days)

Days 61–90 — Own & Close

Full ownership of the merchant journey. Run the entire sales cycle end-to-end. Close and launch your first merchants.

  • Own full cycle: Discovery → Evaluation → Proposal → Close → Launch
  • Close first Closed Won merchant(s) — use the B2B Closed-Won Checklist
  • Launch merchants (0.5% committed GMV realized, active on Shopify)
  • Clean CSM handoffs with full context
  • Hit ramp quota target with 3x coverage
  • Salesforce hygiene: required fields, accurate products, clear notes

Sales Process & Merchant Journey

From the AE Handbook. You own the merchant relationship from inbound lead / qualified opportunity through to launch.

🎯

1. Pre-Qualification

SDRs research and identify high-fit accounts, execute outbound cadences (email, phone, LinkedIn). AEs own inbound leads and qualify for opportunities.

🔍

2. Discovery

Run qualification — fit, authority, need, timing. Document merchant goals/pain points in Salesforce. Identify champion and decision-making process. Use B2B Qualification Questions.

🤝

3. Handoff to AE

SDR provides clear handoff notes (qualification summary, goals, next steps). AE takes ownership, runs deeper discovery, builds business case. Attach partners early via Crossbeam.

🖥️

4. Evaluation

AE owns relationship and sales process. SE owns solution design and technical validation. Collaborate on tailored demos. Navigate champion + economic buyer.

📝

5. Negotiation & Close

Negotiate terms via CPQ. Route non-standard deals through Deal Desk. Contracts via Ironclad (in Salesforce). Use B2B Closed-Won Checklist and B2B Opportunity Valuation job aids.

🚀

6. Onboarding → Launch

Own relationship through launch. Monthly check-ins pre-launch. Collaborate with SE to remove blockers. Launch = 0.5% committed GMV realized. Clean handoff to CSM with documented context.

FAQ

Answers sourced from the AE Handbook and Revenue Onboarding guides.

When should I get my book of business?
In most cases, your manager assigns it early — even as soon as week 2. You don't need to immediately start selling. You're getting familiar with the real accounts you'll be working with. Look for: real competence, team need for coverage, and working tool access.
When do I bring in my SE?
After a qualified discovery call when the merchant has confirmed pain, timeline, and authority. SEs own solution design and technical validation. Collaborate for tailored demos, and attach partners early where possible via Crossbeam.
What does "launch" mean at Shopify?
A merchant is considered launched when they have realized 0.5% committed GMV on Shopify and are active. You own the relationship through launch — maintain monthly check-ins, bridge communication, and collaborate with your SE to remove blockers.
How fast do I need to work inbound leads?
Leads must be processed (Disqualified or Converted to opportunity) within 30 days. Best practice: follow up within 24 hours of a lead becoming "New Sales Ready." Speed to lead = highest conversion.
What's the deal approval process for discounts?
Standard pricing = no approval. Non-standard needs go through Deal Desk (managed within Salesforce via CPQ). Contracts are routed through Ironclad. Ensure Deal Desk/Legal readiness especially around EOQ milestones.
What does my GTM Pod look like?
You work in a cross-functional pod: AE (you), SDR, SE, CSM, Partner Development, and Revenue Marketing. You collaborate across the full merchant journey — tight AE/SE execution, clean CSM handoffs at launch, early partner attachment, and Deal Desk engagement for non-standard deals.
What Salesforce hygiene is expected?
Required fields complete, accurate products, correct opportunity splits. Clear discovery notes, action plans, and next steps on every opportunity. Stage progression should be clean with documented exit criteria.
How do I track my Revenue Onboarding progress?
Use your 1:1s with your manager. Discuss what you're learning, where you're stuck, and what you need. You can also reach out to the Revenue Onboarding team in #help-rev-lnd on Slack for a progress check.
Who do I go to for help?
Your Manager — coaching, strategy, deal reviews
Your Buddy — day-to-day questions, shadowing, introductions
Your SE — technical questions, solution design, demos
Revenue Onboarding team — Slack: #help-rev-lnd
RevOps — Salesforce/tool issues
