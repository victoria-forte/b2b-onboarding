Technical Concepts

Companies (B2B Primitive)

Foundational data structure for B2B in Shopify
⚠️ CRITICAL FOR BILLING — Getting this wrong means merchants overpay.

Structure

  • Company = Parent organization for one or more locations
  • Company Location = The actual business you're selling to
  • Customer = Individual person who purchases on behalf of the business

Why It's Critical

  • Orders tied to a Company → billed at B2B VPF rate (0.18%)
  • Orders NOT tied to a Company → billed at DTC VPF rate (0.40%) — merchant overpays!
  • Required for proper GMV classification in billing system

Red Flags (Implementation Errors)

  • ❌ Tagging customers as "B2B" instead of using Companies primitive
  • ❌ Using discount apps for B2B pricing instead of proper catalog assignment
  • ❌ Custom functions without Company association
#b2b-primitive #billing

Processes & Rituals

PMF Framework (Product Market Fit)

Assess whether an opportunity is a good fit for Shopify B2B

Scoring System

  • Categories: 1, 2, 3, 4
  • Category 2 or 3: Requires Callum's approval to proceed
  • PMF value of 4: CANNOT move to Closed Won — automatic disqualification

Process

  1. Complete technical assessment
  2. For deals >$10M: Upload to NotebookLM
  3. Ask Sales Assistant for PMF analysis
  4. Work through with Callum
  5. Remove source from NotebookLM
#process #qualification

4 Non-Negotiable Qualification Pillars

ALL FOUR MUST BE MET to qualify a B2B opportunity

1. Business Intent ✅

Looking for:

  • Business wants to use Shopify to originate and/or manage orders
  • Self-serve B2B buying experience OR manual through Shopify admin
  • If orders won't be brokered, they must transact on Shopify

Red Flags:

  • ❌ Not interested in order origination or transaction processing
  • ❌ Don't want customers to login (DTC play, not B2B)

2. Revenue Opportunity 💰

Looking for:

  • B2B-specific GMV through Shopify ≥ $5M annually
Example Disqualification: $10M B2B company bringing only $3M to platform = DISQUALIFIED

3. Technical Fit 🔧

Looking for:

  • Manageable technical landscape
  • Straightforward product catalog
  • Known integrations with existing tech stack

⚠️ Yellow Flags:

  • Complex pricing requirements
  • Configurable products
  • ERPs without known connector

Red Flags:

  • ❌ EDI/Punchout is a hard line — if majority relies on EDI, NOT a fit
  • ❌ Small % EDI okay IF they understand it lives outside Shopify

4. Urgency ⏰

Looking for:

  • Decision in next month
  • Implementation within 4–6 months

Red Flags:

  • ❌ Decision 1–2 years from now = nurture lead, not active opp
#process #qualification #non-negotiable

Order Origination Framework

Calculate how much GMV will actually flow through Shopify

Why It Exists

Company revenue is a vanity metric — we must know the on-platform potential.

Key Discovery Questions

  • Do you sell via EDI? Punchout? POS?
  • What's your estimated revenue through EDI/Punchout/POS?
  • What's your total annual B2B revenue?

Two Calculation Methods

Top-Down: Start with total B2B revenue, subtract non-platform channels to find on-platform GMV potential.

Business & Product Context

VPF (Variable Platform Fee)

Core billing mechanic for Shopify B2B

Rates

  • B2B: 0.18% (18 basis points)
  • DTC: 0.40% (40 basis points)

Impact Example

Merchant processes $10M in B2B GMV:

✅ Properly tagged to Companies: $10M × 0.18% = $18,000 VPF
❌ Incorrectly tagged (DTC):    $10M × 0.40% = $40,000 VPF
💸 Cost of error: $22,000 overpayment

#billing #pricing #b2b-primitive

Billable Orders

When does Shopify bill VPF on an order?

Two Key Questions

  1. Was the order brokered by Shopify?
  2. Was the transaction (payment) brokered by Shopify?

If EITHER = YES → Billable

✅ Billable

  • Online store orders (paid at checkout, invoice, marked as paid)
  • Draft orders (paid immediately, via invoice, or marked as paid)
  • Imported orders paid in Shopify

❌ NOT Billable

  • Orders not marked as paid
  • Imported orders already marked as paid before import
  • Orders marked as paid only in ERP (not Shopify)
#billing #orders

Leaky Monetization

Structural Issue #1 Preventing Scale
⚠️ Definition: When Shopify doesn't get paid for GMV flowing through the platform.

Root Cause

Order starts in Shopify → goes to ERP → NOT marked as paid in Shopify = $0 VPF

Prevention

  • Ensure partner builds mark orders as paid in Shopify
  • Set clear expectations: "Close out orders on Shopify"
  • Qualify on workflow during discovery
#billing #qualification

EDI (Electronic Data Interchange)

Computer-to-computer exchange of business documents

Problem

  • EDI orders skip Shopify entirely
  • EDI revenue = $0 Billable GMV = $0 VPF

Qualification

  • 🚨 Majority EDI = NOT a fit
  • Small % okay IF merchant understands it lives outside Shopify

Discovery Questions

  • "Do you sell via EDI?"
  • "What percentage of orders/revenue through EDI?"
#technical #qualification

ERP Integrations

64% of B2B launches DELAYED due to ERP integrations

Discovery Questions

  • "Will orders originate in ERP or Shopify?"
  • "What system is Source of Truth?"
  • "What's your current ERP?"

Risk Assessment

  • ✅ Green: Known connector, Shopify is Source of Truth
  • ⚠️ Yellow: No known connector, complex sync needs
  • 🚨 Red: ERP is Source of Truth + orders marked as paid only in ERP
#technical #qualification

ICP Definition

Current Ideal Customer Profile (after team refocus)

1. B2B First

  • Primary business model is B2B
  • May have small D2C component

2. DTC Wholesalers

  • Started as DTC brand
  • Now adding wholesale/B2B

NOT the ICP

  • ❌ Primarily D2C or Only D2C
  • ❌ Manufacturers focused on industrial/construction
  • ❌ Heavy EDI-dependent businesses
#icp #qualification

Discovery Questions — Quick Reference

Critical Qualification Questions

Use these in every discovery call
  1. "Do you sell via EDI? What percentage?" — Revenue Qualification
  2. "What's your total annual B2B revenue?" — Revenue Qualification
  3. "Do you sell both D2C and B2B from same storefront?" — ICP Identification
  4. "Are B2B buyers required to login?" — Business Intent
  5. "When orders are placed, will they originate in ERP or Shopify?" — Technical Fit
  6. "Do you offer payment terms? What % of buyers on terms?" — Good Fit Signal
  7. "What's your current ERP/tech stack?" — Integration Complexity

Red Flags Cheat Sheet

🚨 Hard Disqualifications

  • ❌ Majority of business relies on EDI
  • ❌ PMF score of 4
  • ❌ On-platform GMV < $5M
  • ❌ Don't want customers to login
  • ❌ Not interested in order origination on Shopify
  • ❌ Decision timeline 1–2 years out

⚠️ Yellow Flags — Proceed with Caution

  • Complex pricing requirements
  • Configurable products
  • ERP without known connector
  • Early evaluation stage (decision in 1–3 months)
  • Flexible implementation timeline (6–12 months)

✅ Green Flags — Good Fit

  • High % of buyers on payment terms
  • Looking to replace disparate systems
  • Decision within 1 month
  • Implementation within 4–6 months
  • Straightforward product catalog
  • Known ERP connector exists
  • Small/manageable EDI percentage
