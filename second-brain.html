Technical Concepts

Companies (B2B Primitive)

Foundational data structure for B2B in Shopify
⚠️ CRITICAL FOR BILLING — Getting this wrong means merchants overpay.

Structure

  • Company = Parent organization for one or more locations
  • Company Location = The actual business you're selling to
  • Customer = Individual person who purchases on behalf of the business

Why It's Critical

  • Orders tied to a Company → billed at B2B VPF rate (0.18%)
  • Orders NOT tied to a Company → billed at DTC VPF rate (0.40%) — merchant overpays!
  • Required for proper GMV classification in billing system

Red Flags (Implementation Errors)

  • ❌ Tagging customers as "B2B" instead of using Companies primitive
  • ❌ Using discount apps for B2B pricing instead of proper catalog assignment
  • ❌ Custom functions without Company association
#b2b-primitive #billing

Catalogs

Tailor pricing and product availability to every customer, in any currency

How It Works

Processes & Rituals

PMF Framework (Product Market Fit)

Assess whether an opportunity is a good fit for Shopify B2B

Scoring System

  • Categories: 1, 2, 3, 4
  • Category 2 or 3: Requires Callum's approval to proceed
  • PMF value of 4: CANNOT move to Closed Won — automatic disqualification

Process

  1. Complete technical assessment
  2. For deals >$10M: Upload to NotebookLM
  3. Ask Sales Assistant for PMF analysis
  4. Work through with Callum
  5. Remove source from NotebookLM
#process #qualification

4 Non-Negotiable Qualification Pillars

ALL FOUR MUST BE MET to qualify a B2B opportunity

1. Business Intent ✅

Looking for:

  • Business wants to use Shopify to originate and/or manage orders
  • Self-serve B2B buying experience OR manual through Shopify admin
  • If orders won't be brokered, they must transact on Shopify

Red Flags:

  • ❌ Not interested in order origination or transaction processing
  • ❌ Don't want customers to login (DTC play, not B2B)

2. Revenue Opportunity 💰

Looking for:

  • B2B-specific GMV through Shopify ≥ $5M annually
Example Disqualification: $10M B2B company bringing only $3M to platform = DISQUALIFIED

3. Technical Fit 🔧

Looking for:

  • Manageable technical landscape
  • Straightforward product catalog
  • Known integrations with existing tech stack

⚠️ Yellow Flags:

  • Complex pricing requirements
  • Configurable products
  • ERPs without known connector

Red Flags:

  • ❌ EDI/Punchout is a hard line — if majority relies on EDI, NOT a fit
  • ❌ Small % EDI okay IF they understand it lives outside Shopify

4. Urgency ⏰

Looking for:

  • Decision in next month
  • Implementation within 4–6 months

Red Flags:

  • ❌ Decision 1–2 years from now = nurture lead, not active opp
#process #qualification #non-negotiable

Order Origination Framework

Calculate how much GMV will actually flow through Shopify

Why It Exists

Company revenue is a vanity metric — we must know the on-platform potential.

Key Discovery Questions

  • Do you sell via EDI? Punchout? POS?
  • What's your estimated revenue through EDI/Punchout/POS?
  • What's your total annual B2B revenue?

Two Calculation Methods

Top-Down: Start with total B2B revenue, subtract non-platform channels to find on-platform GMV potential.

Business & Product Context

VPF (Variable Platform Fee)

Core billing mechanic for Shopify B2B

Rates

  • B2B VPF: 0.18% — applied when orders are tied to a Company
  • DTC VPF: 0.40% — applied when orders are NOT tied to a Company

Formula

VPF = GMV × VPF Rate
B2B Example: $5M GMV × 0.18% = $9,000
DTC Example: $5M GMV × 0.40% = $20,000

Proper Companies implementation directly impacts merchant billing accuracy and Shopify's revenue classification. Getting this wrong means merchants overpay by more than 2×.
