💰 Pricing Objections

"Shopify Plus is too expensive"

Answer

"Let's talk total cost of ownership, not just platform fees. Our customers save an average of 23% on platform costs and 33% on implementation compared to competitors."

Proof Points

  • Adobe Commerce: 42% higher platform costs
  • BigCommerce: 32% higher platform costs, 88% higher implementation costs
  • WooCommerce: 41% higher operating costs

"You're not just paying for software—you get 300+ new features per year at no extra cost. Most platforms charge for major feature releases."

ROI Reframe

"Instead of focusing on cost, let's calculate the value. If self-service B2B ordering reduces your cost-to-serve by $40K/year and lifts conversion 8%, what's the payback period on your investment?"

Use the "Calculate the Value of your B2B Opportunity" framework (available in B2B Product Hub)

"We can't justify the ROI"

Answer

"Merchants who re-platform to Shopify see 15% incremental revenue on average. What would a 15% lift mean for your business?"

  • Time to value: Implementations are 20% faster and you're 66% more likely to launch on time
  • Eliminate manual order processing: $40K+/year
  • Consolidate systems (separate B2B + DTC platforms): $80K+/year in platform fees + dev
  • Reduce custom development: 70% reduction in custom code

Ask Back

"What's the cost of not changing? How much revenue are you leaving on the table with your current limitations?"

"Unlike platforms that require custom code for every integration, we have pre-built connectors that reduce integration time by 50–70%."

"Winning the Integration Conversation" (4:27 min) — B2B Bites

"Our needs are too complex / We need heavy customization"

Answer

"I'd challenge that assumption. What we see is that 70% of 'custom' requirements are actually solved by out-of-the-box features or existing apps."

"Let's walk through your requirements. Often what required custom dev on your old platform is native in Shopify—like payment terms, volume pricing, or company accounts."

"And where you do need customization, our platform is built for it—Shopify Functions let you extend checkout, payment, and fulfillment logic without touching core code."

Reframe

"The question isn't 'Can Shopify do what our current platform does?' It's 'What can Shopify help us do that we can't do today?'"
"We need [specific feature] that Shopify doesn't have"

Answer

"Let's talk about that. What's the business problem you're trying to solve?" (Dig into the why behind the feature)

"Often there's an alternative approach that achieves the same outcome. For example, if you need subscription billing for B2B, we have apps like Recharge or Bold that handle that."

"We also ship 300+ features per year. What's on your 'must-have' list? I can check the roadmap or escalate it to our product team."

If It's a Real Gap

"You're right, we don't have [feature] natively today. But here's what we do have [alternative solution]. Would that solve 80% of your need?"

"I can also connect you with our Solutions Engineering team to explore custom solutions or app integrations."

Answer

"BigCommerce is a solid platform, but here's where we see merchants switch: native B2B features. On BigCommerce, you need apps like Bundleb2b or custom dev for features like company accounts, payment terms, and checkout to draft—those are built into Shopify."

  • Implementation cost: BigCommerce implementations cost 88% more than Shopify on average
  • Case study: Gesswein was on BigCommerce and switched specifically for better ERP integration and B2B workflows. They migrated in 6 months.

"We need enterprise-grade like Magento / Adobe Commerce"

Answer

"I get it—Magento has a reputation for 'enterprise.' But let's talk about what that really means: high total cost of ownership."

  • Cost: Adobe Commerce has 42% higher platform costs and 24% higher operating costs
  • Complexity: Most Magento merchants spend more time maintaining custom code than innovating. Merchants tell us they eliminated 70% of their custom code by moving to Shopify.
  • Recognition: Forrester Leader in B2B Commerce (2024) and Gartner Leader in Digital Commerce (3 years running)

"We're enterprise-grade, but without the enterprise headache."

Answer

"SFCC is powerful, but it's built for massive enterprise retail, not mid-market B2B. Here's the difference:"

  • Implementation: SFCC takes 12–18 months. Shopify is 3–6 months.
  • Cost: SFCC implementations run $500K–$2M+. You'll spend more on implementation than you will on Shopify in 5 years.
  • Checkout: Our checkout converts up to 36% better than SFCC according to third-party benchmarks.

"SFCC makes sense for Fortune 100 retailers. For B2B, you'll spend less and launch faster with Shopify."

  • B2B: We've shipped 45+ B2B features since 2022. Forrester Leader in B2B Commerce as of 2024.

"Shopify doesn't support subscriptions / EDI / advanced workflows"

Answer

  • Subscriptions: Best-in-class apps like Recharge, Bold, and Skio. Native integration, complex subscription logic.
  • EDI: Partners like SPS Commerce and TrueCommerce handle EDI transactions — 850s, 810s, 856s, etc.
  • Advanced workflows: Shopify Flow automates complex workflows — approval processes, inventory rules, customer tagging. Visual, no-code automation.

Reframe

"We build the core commerce platform and let best-in-class partners build specialized functionality. That means you get the best EDI solution, not a mediocre one built by a commerce platform."
"We need multi-currency / multi-language / international"

Answer

  • We support 20+ currencies and 133+ countries out of the box
  • Shopify Markets handles international expansion — localized pricing, duties/taxes, local payment methods, translated storefronts
  • For B2B: Markets + Catalogs (launched 2025) — manage international B2B customers with region-specific pricing and products

⏱️ Urgency / Timing Objections

"We're not ready to make a change right now"

Answer

"Here's the risk of waiting: every month on your current platform is lost opportunity. If we could help you grow revenue by 15% or reduce costs by $50K/year, how much is waiting costing you?"

Create Urgency

🛠️ Objection Handling Frameworks

The 4-Step Objection Framework

  1. Acknowledge: "I understand where you're coming from."
  2. Clarify: "Can you tell me more about [objection]?"
  3. Respond: Address with proof points, case studies, ROI
  4. Confirm: "Does that address your concern?"

The FEEL-FELT-FOUND Method

  • "I understand how you feel."
  • "Other merchants have felt the same way."
  • "What they found was [insert success story]."

Example: "I understand how you feel about migration risk. Other merchants felt the same way before switching. What they found was that with our partner ecosystem and phased approach, they launched on time with zero downtime—and unlocked new revenue streams they couldn't do before."

💡 Pro Tips

  1. Never dodge an objection — Acknowledge it head-on
  2. Use proof points — Stats, case studies, analyst validation
  3. Reframe — Turn objections into opportunities ("That's exactly why merchants switch")
  4. Ask back — "What would it take to solve that concern?"
  5. Third-party validate — Forrester, Gartner, customer testimonials
  6. ROI over features — Always tie back to business outcomes
